# Avon: From Mum's Makeup to Must-Have

## The problem

Avon had a perception problem. A beloved brand with decades of history, it was struggling to connect with a younger generation of customers who saw it as their mum's makeup. The digital product offering was strong but the brand voice and editorial positioning weren't doing it justice. And on top of repositioning an iconic brand, the annual breast cancer fundraising campaign needed to cut through in a crowded cause-marketing space and drive real donations, not just awareness.

## What I built

* Bridged the print and digital marketing teams to align voice and strategy across both channels, ending the disconnect between how Avon looked in print and how it showed up online

* Developed a new editorial tone: fashion-forward, BFF-warm, upbeat — desirable without the hard sell

* Created and launched a fashion editorial destination from zero — trend pieces, product pages, beauty editor notes — positioning Avon as a trusted style source for a new generation

* Built a data-driven business case for the editorial investment and spearheaded creative and issue planning

* Produced supporting eDM content, 600+ SEO product descriptions across seven categories, and a product microsite

* Led the Avon Breast Cancer Crusade campaign from brief through to full execution — shaping the campaign narrative, not just writing the copy

* Developed the campaign voice: sincere, motivational, personal — leading with real stories rather than statistics

* Wrote and integrated messaging across every channel: brochure, PR, event, web, email and social

* Created the recruitment hook that activated the Avon representative network as campaign ambassadors

## The outcomes

Fashion editorial destination launched without budget — averaged 124 visits per day, 5% newsletter conversion, $30 average order basket, top 3 organic search results. Content directly attributable to sales uplift across digital channels.

Breast Cancer Crusade campaign raised $1M for breast cancer research.
